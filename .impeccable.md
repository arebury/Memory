# Impeccable · Design Context

Synthesised from `src/imports/pasted_text/memory.md` (sec 1, 4, 5, 16) and the live design system in `src/styles/sc-design-system.css`. Authoritative for any future `/impeccable craft|extract` invocation in this repo.

## Users

Contact-center **supervisors and platform administrators** working in front of a desktop dashboard during their shift. Their context is professional, focused, often noisy: they monitor live conversation streams, audit selected interactions, and configure automation rules. They are not casual users — they spend hours/day in this app and need information density without overwhelm.

Primary jobs to be done:
- Filter / locate a specific conversation across a high-volume dataset.
- Launch transcription or AI-analysis on one or many conversations at once.
- Configure rules that automate recording / transcription / classification.
- Review the resulting transcript + AI summary + sentiment of any one conversation.

## Brand Personality

Three words: **professional, precise, calm**.

Voice: instructional but not bureaucratic — short Spanish UI strings, lowercase where possible (`genera coste`, `todo procesado`, `admiten análisis`), uppercase only for short structural labels (`TOTAL A PROCESAR`, `ANÁLISIS`).

This is a tool for someone whose job depends on reading dense data fast. Whitespace and rhythm matter more than decoration. Noise is the enemy.

## Aesthetic Direction

The app is governed by the in-house **Smart Contact Design System** (tokens in `sc-design-system.css`, Figma source `Smart-Contact---Design-System`):

- **Theme**: light. Reason: 8-hour daytime usage by supervisors in office lighting. Dark mode is roadmap (see `memory.md` sec 17), not current.
- **Surfaces**: warm-tinted neutrals (`#FFFFFF`, `#F9FAFB`, `#F3F4F6`, `#D3D5DA`). Hairline dividers `--sc-border-soft` (#F3F4F6); container borders `--sc-border-default` (#D3D5DA).
- **Brand accent**: teal `--sc-accent` (#60D3E4) and the stronger `--sc-accent-strong` (#48B8C9). Used sparingly — confirmation, processed-state cues, primary-action highlights. The 60-30-10 rule is in force; teal is the 10%.
- **Cost / risk colour**: amber `--sc-cost-warn` (#D97706). Reserved for "this generates cost" or "this is irreversible" cues.
- **Display number**: `text-sc-emphasis` (#3C434D — softened black, never #000) at 88–112px in the bulk transcription modal hero.
- **Typography**: Roboto (300/400/500/700). Yes, this is on the reflex-rejection list — but the design system was built before this skill existed and Roboto is the locked client choice. Until the cliente's DS migrates (sec 16), Roboto stays. New additions must NOT bring in a second font without an explicit DS decision.
- **Iconography**: `lucide-react` for product icons; project-supplied SVGs for status pictograms (`StatusIcons.tsx`) where the DS provides assets.
- **Numbers**: `tabular-nums` everywhere a number can change in place (counts, durations, timestamps).
- **Animation**: SC-prefixed micro-animations only (`animate-sc-pulse`, `animate-sc-bump`, `animate-sc-shake`, `animate-sc-delta-fly`). 200–360 ms, easing `ease-out`. No bounce, no elastic.

## Design Principles

1. **Density over chrome.** Supervisors live in a table all day. Add information per pixel; never decoration for decoration's sake.
2. **Tokens first.** Every padding, color, font-size, animation has a `--sc-*` token. Hard-coded magic numbers in JSX are tech debt — open a token in `sc-design-system.css`.
3. **One status pictogram, not three badges.** When a row's processing state can be encoded in a single icon (channel + transcription + analysis), do that. Stacks of badges in 80 px columns are noise.
4. **Reserve teal for confirmation.** `text-sc-accent-strong` is for processed states, primary CTAs, and the "ON" state of high-impact toggles. Never for body text, never for decoration.
5. **No AI tells.** No gradient text, no `border-left: 3px solid` accent stripes, no card-grid-of-identical-cards, no purple/cyan glow on dark, no glassmorphism layered on solid surfaces. The app should look like enterprise software a careful designer made, not like a generated mock.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS dashboards with rainbow KPI cards.
- Fintech "chat with AI" launchpads (gradient hero + giant rounded button).
- Anything that screams "shadcn default theme out of the box" without intervention.
